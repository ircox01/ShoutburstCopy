Will the last person working turn out the lights

We don’t mean to be alarmist and the headline might be slightly confusing because many more people are in work and everything is starting to look rosy.  I am referring to the expected increase in over 65’s by 20% in a decade.  The whole country is turning grey.

[Daily Mail article]

Now, I don’t want to be labelled as ageist, blue-rinsist or slow queue-ist.  I like and respect older people and know that they are an important part of society.  And I have a Mum & Dad to prove it.

But there are serious issues for us in the customer service game to consider.  Older people prefer to use the telephone.  Older people are less likely to use social media, mobile applications and web-chat.  Older people are more likely to request Frank Sinatra as hold music.  Ok, I made the last one up.
Care should be taken to think long and hard about the way we respond to the needs of a changing population

The point is that call centre managers need to be aware of this change and be ready to respond to it.  So rather than a headlong flight into multi-media, multi-channel and multi-problem solutions, care should be taken to think long and hard about the way we respond to the needs of a changing population.  So don’t ditch the phones and be prepared to work with the older generation while your competitors put up technological barriers.  Because the pounds may be grey but there are lots of them and there will soon be 20% more floating around.


You have been warned. And if you don’t do what we say, we’ll send the old boys around.
