Customers fighting back – hooray

Everyone has their worst nightmare.  For me it involves cliffs and sharks, and hopefully if I stay well away from the sea it will remain just a nightmare.  For the average Call Centre Manager, it is likely to either be 4000 calls in the queue with all agents at lunch, or being strapped down in a chair and made to listen to customer complaints.  And for the pessimist amongst us, both.

According to the news that latter nightmare is more likely than ever

http://www.corpcommsmagazine.co.uk/news/3672-more-uk-consumers-are-standing-up-for-customer-service-standards

Customers are standing up for their customer service rights.  Now, I am not 100 sure what a ‘customer service right’ is.  The right to have your name pronounced correctly?  The right to never be cut off during a heated conversation?  The right to speak to a supervisor when requested rather than being told the supervisor doesn’t have a mind of their own or that the agent in question is suddenly the manager in charge, and everyone else has mysteriously disappeared.  What a co-incidence.
One angry customer will tell eighteen others, including the butcher, the baker etc.

But back to the point.  Customers are telling it how it is.  More often and probably louder.  But this is a good thing.  It is.  Yes, it is.  Do you want me to shout?  It is.  Because if customers that are not happy are also not good for business.  We all know the statistics.  One angry customer will tell eighteen others, including the butcher, the baker etc. And then they will leave and buy everything elsewhere.  In summary, not good.  But dealing with the customer complaint well is very much the flipside.  Customers love us – and we have learnt something.

So when customers shout at us listen carefully, fight the urge to shout ‘hooray’ and then use the information to the best of your ability to improve.  Done well, there will be less shouting but more business.  Done badly, you will have a real fight on your hands.
