Give your phone number, you fools…

Why is it so difficult to find a phone number when you need it?  Some of the biggest brands have been in the news recently accused of hiding phone numbers in the smallest of the small print. Why would they do this to their customers?

Well, of course there are other ways.  Using the website to search for the answers you need is one ay although for too many sites the questions and answers seem to bear little resemblance to reality.  Why would I want to know that I can email a question when I want to complain about their email service in the first place? Sometimes it is good to give a company a public shaming on social media, especially is they deserve it.  Tweet something dramatic, retweet it to everyone you know and stand back for the results.  Child’s play.

Or send SMS or email and hope that they appear on someone’s desk at some stage this side of Christmas.

But for many transactions, the humble phone call is still the best way of communicating and big business would be wise to bear that in mind.  For a phone call includes the world of relationship building, upselling and problem solving unlike any other.  It also includes long queues, agents who seem to struggle to stay awake and phrases like ‘it’s company’ policy but let’s ignore the downsides for now when the upsides are more appealing and pertinent to the article.

Give your customers the phone number, sort out your processes and work with your agents to look at how to add value with every call.  It might not be cheap but done right it will bring far more value to your organisation.  Of course, if you do it badly then you might as well.
