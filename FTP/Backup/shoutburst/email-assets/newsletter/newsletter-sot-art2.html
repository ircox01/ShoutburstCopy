Starbucks charging mats matter

Many customer service stories make you groan and say ‘no’.  At least they do to me. So when a story comes along that makes you feel good and shout the opposite, it is worth writing about.

Starbucks announced recently that after providing free wifi at all of their outlets, they would go one further and add charging mats at each table, which would allow the average coffee drinking, laptop toting, mobile brandishing workaholic commuter unlimited charging and make them very happy indeed.  And it is good news to people like me who, whenever I visit one of these places, try to obtain the table nearest the one plug socket in the room or (and this is not a recommendation) drape your extra-long charging cable across three tables, a baby in a pram and three mothers arguing about which child is the most advanced.  We will not be welcome again, I fear.
The positive customer service wave will be ridden for a while, especially as people like me write about it.

But it is not just power that Starbucks is turning on.  The publicity itself must have been worth several million beans, and the positive customer service wave will be ridden for a while, especially as people like me write about it.

What a great idea.  Thanks should go to Starbucks for allowing me to now spend all day drinking one cup of coffee, rather than the 2.7 hours it would take my battery to die.  Thanks should also go for being grown up about providing computer access.  Great Western Railways, take note.  But most of all, thanks for providing some light relief from a positive story and a very good idea.  I’ll say ‘cheers’ over my cup of mocha and a sugared croissant.
